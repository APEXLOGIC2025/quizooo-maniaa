Quiz Hosting
How to run a live quiz for 50+ people without any technical setup

Running a live quiz for a large group used to require expensive AV equipment, dedicated software licences and technical staff. Browser-based platforms have changed this completely — here is how to host a professional quiz session for any group size with nothing more than a laptop and a projector.

What you need

The minimum setup for a QuizArena session is a single device (laptop or desktop) connected to a display screen. Players use their own smartphones to participate — no additional hardware is needed. For larger audiences:

  • Small groups (up to 20): A laptop screen turned outward, or a TV with HDMI connection
  • Medium groups (20-80): A projector or large display with the host screen visible to everyone
  • Large events (80+): A projector with sound system for game music, and a second screen for the waiting room display

Preparing your questions

The best quiz questions are specific, unambiguous and appropriate for your audience. Avoid questions that depend on very recent news (unless the quiz is specifically about current events) and avoid questions where multiple options could be argued as correct.

QuizArena accepts Excel files in .xlsx format. Your file needs four columns: Question, Option A, Option B, Option C, Option D, and Correct Answer (the letter A, B, C or D). Name the file clearly and test it with a small session before your main event.

Setting up the room

Before players arrive, display the waiting screen on the projector. This shows the room code and QR code prominently. Brief your audience on how to join — most participants scan the QR code faster than typing the room code manually. Have the room code written somewhere visible as a backup for those who prefer to type it.

During the quiz

Use the pause feature between questions to add commentary, build tension or give clues about the next topic. The game music handles atmosphere automatically. After each question, briefly acknowledge the top three scorers to keep the competitive energy high. For auction rounds, encourage players to discuss their bids aloud — this creates significant audience engagement.

Ending the session

The final leaderboard is the climax of the quiz. Display it for at least two minutes and acknowledge the top scorers. Export the session data for your records — QuizArena logs all session details in your host dashboard for review after the event.

Question Design
Writing quiz questions that are fair, engaging and impossible to argue with

The quality of a quiz depends entirely on the quality of its questions. Poorly written questions lead to arguments, disengagement and a bad experience for everyone. Here is a practical guide to writing questions that work perfectly in a live competitive setting.

The anatomy of a good quiz question

A good quiz question has exactly one correct answer that everyone can agree on, three plausible wrong answers that test whether participants actually know the subject, and clear unambiguous language with no room for interpretation. Every word in the question matters.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Double negatives: "Which of the following is NOT an incorrect statement about..." — confusing and error-prone. Keep phrasing positive where possible.
  • Trick questions: Questions designed to catch participants on technicalities rather than test knowledge create frustration, not engagement.
  • Ambiguous options: "All of the above" or "None of the above" as options is generally poor practice in live quizzes — it slows down decision-making.
  • Too easy or too hard: Aim for a mix of difficulty — approximately 30% easy (confidence builders), 50% medium and 20% challenging.

Categories that work well

For general audience quizzes, the most engaging categories are: geography, history, science and technology, entertainment and popular culture, sports, and current affairs. Avoid highly specialised topics unless your audience has a specific subject expertise.

Using auction rounds strategically

Auction bidding rounds work best with questions where confidence varies significantly among participants — topics where some people are experts and others have no idea. Science facts, niche history, and statistics questions work particularly well. Avoid using auction rounds for questions that most participants either all know or all do not know.

Testing your questions before the event

Run a test session with a small group of 3-5 people before any major event. This surfaces ambiguous wording, incorrect correct answers and questions that are too easy or too hard. A 5-minute test session can save significant embarrassment during the live event.

5 ways schools and colleges in India are using live quiz platforms in 2025

Educational institutions across India are increasingly integrating live quiz tools into classroom and event settings. Here are five specific ways QuizArena and similar platforms are being used effectively in the Indian education sector.

1. Classroom revision sessions

Teachers use live quizzes as end-of-chapter revision tools. Students answer on their phones while the teacher controls the pace. The competitive element significantly increases engagement compared to traditional revision methods — participation rates in quiz-based revision sessions are consistently higher than written assessments.

2. Annual day and cultural events

School and college annual days increasingly feature live quiz competitions as a highlight event. QuizArena's game show atmosphere — with music, countdown timers and dramatic leaderboard reveals — suits large auditorium settings with projected displays. The auction bidding format is particularly popular for inter-house competitions.

3. Orientation and induction programmes

New student orientation programmes use quiz formats to introduce institutional history, policies and campus information in an engaging way. Students absorb information much more effectively when it is framed as a game rather than a lecture — and the competitive format encourages students to pay attention to orientation materials in advance.

Interschool competitions traditionally required dedicated quiz software, AV teams and printed question sheets. Browser-based platforms reduce this overhead dramatically — a single laptop and internet connection is sufficient. Multiple schools can participate simultaneously from their own devices with a single shared room code.

Corporate Events
Using live quizzes for corporate team building and training — a practical guide

Team quizzes are one of the most consistently effective team building activities because they combine collaboration, competition and shared experience. Here is how to design and run a successful quiz-based team building session in a corporate environment.

Individual vs team formats

For team building purposes, a team format works better than individual competition — teams of 3-5 discuss answers before submitting, creating genuine collaboration. Designate one team member as the answer submitter and rotate this role across questions to ensure everyone is involved.

Question categories for corporate quizzes

The best corporate quiz question mix combines company-specific knowledge (products, history, values, recent milestones), industry knowledge, and general knowledge. Avoid questions that put individuals on the spot or reveal knowledge gaps that could be embarrassing. The goal is to celebrate collective intelligence, not expose individual weaknesses.

Using auction rounds for training

Auction bidding rounds work exceptionally well in training contexts. A question about a company policy or product feature, where teams must bid based on their confidence before seeing the options, naturally reveals which teams have absorbed training content and which have gaps — without singling out individuals. Trainers find the audit data useful for identifying areas that need reinforcement.

Hybrid events — remote and in-person

QuizArena works equally well for fully remote teams or hybrid events. Remote participants join on their own devices exactly as in-person participants do. The host can run the session from any location — the platform does not require all participants to be in the same physical space.

After the quiz

Export the session data from the host dashboard and share the leaderboard results with the team via email or the company communication channel. Recognising quiz performance publicly — even informally — reinforces the team building value and creates positive associations with future sessions.
